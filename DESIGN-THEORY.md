# ZenSpace Design Theory Framework
*Sourced from Dustin's NotebookLM research corpus on interior design theory*

## 1. Aesthetic Order & Simplicity
- **Architectural Integrity** over superficial decoration (Wharton & Codman)
- Sacrifice the "showy" and "vulgar" for harmony, balance, symmetry, order
- **Simplicity & Proportion**: Open, uncluttered spaces; well-made furniture over abundance
- Relate objects to room's scale — avoid "stuffing" spaces
- **Visual Logic**: Essentiality, reduction of "perceptual overload" through clean lines
- Aligns with Universal Design's "Simple and Intuitive Use"

## 2. Human-Centric & Functional (Anthropometry & Ergonomics)
- **Fit & Clearance**: Dynamic dimensions (people in motion), not just static
- Clearance based on 95th percentile; reach based on 5th percentile
- **The "Average" Fallacy**: Don't design for average — design for adjustable range or extremes
- **Proxemics**: Respect social zones (intimate, personal, social, public distances) in furniture placement

## 3. Universal & Inclusive Design (7 Principles)
- **Equitable Use**: Useful to people with diverse abilities
- **Perceptible Information**: Contrast and lighting to define edges/pathways
- **Low Physical Effort**: Neutral body position, minimize fatigue
- **Size & Space for Approach**: Adequate reach regardless of body size or mobility aids

## 4. Biophilic & Regenerative Design
- **Light & Space**: Varied lighting (light pools, filtered daylight), not uniform flat lighting
- **Natural Shapes & Forms**: Organic forms (curves, arches) to soften and reduce stress
- **Prospect & Refuge**: Balance openness with enclosure/safety
- **Systemic Regeneration**: Materials aligned with circularity, low-embodied carbon

## 5. Phenomenological Principles ("Feel" of Space)
- **Genius Loci** (Spirit of Place): Preserve identity — orientation, light quality, structural "bones"
- Don't apply generic templates that erase context
- **Multi-Sensory**: Acoustics, texture, touch (haptics)
- "Clean" design should not mean poor acoustics; choose materials for tactile warmth and sound absorption

---

## Application to ZenSpace
These frameworks should guide:
- **Room analysis**: Camera/photo analysis should identify existing spatial character (genius loci)
- **Design suggestions**: Grounded in these 5 frameworks, not just aesthetic preference
- **Room saving**: Users build holistic spaces over time, guided by principles
- **UI language**: Surface these concepts as educational moments (tooltips, principle cards)
